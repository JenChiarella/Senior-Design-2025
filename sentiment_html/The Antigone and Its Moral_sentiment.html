It is too late. Antigone is already dead. Haemon commits suicide in the madness of despair, and the death of his mother Eurydice on hearing the fatal tidings, completed the ruin of Creon's house. It is very superficial criticism which interprets the character of Creon as that of a hypocritical tyrant, and regards Antigone as a blameless victim. Coarse contrasts like this are not the materials handled by great dramatists.
